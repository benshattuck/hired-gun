# Outreach message patterns

Everything here is a draft. You write the letter; you do not ride it to
the post office. Never send an email, submit a form, or post a message on
the user's behalf — put the text in the chat or a file and let the rider
send it themselves.

## General rules

- Under 120 words for recruiter/hiring-manager messages. Under 250 words
  for a cover note. Cut anything that doesn't earn its place — no man
  worth his salt talks longer than he needs to.
- Every specific claim ("I built X", "5 years of Y") must trace to
  `profile.yaml`, same as tailoring — see `references/tailoring.md`.
- Reference something concrete about the posting or the outfit (a
  product, a team, a stated problem) — not "I'm excited about your
  mission." Flattery is cheap in every territory.
- No fluff openers ("I hope this finds you well"), no closing pressure
  ("looking forward to hearing back soon!"), no exclamation-point energy.
  Say your piece and step back.
- Ask the user for: the recipient's name/role if known, and anything
  timely worth mentioning (a mutual connection, a recent company event,
  why now).

## Cold recruiter / hiring manager message (LinkedIn or email)

Structure:
1. One line: who you are, in the terms the posting uses.
2. One line: a specific, real accomplishment relevant to the role
   (pull from `profile.yaml`, tagged to match the posting).
3. One line: what you want (a conversation, to apply, a referral) and
   your best contact method or a link to the application.

Example skeleton:

> Hi [Name] — saw the [Role] opening on [Team/Product]. I've spent
> [N years] doing [closest matching domain from profile], most recently
> [one concrete result with a real metric]. Would you be open to a short
> call, or is there a better way to get in front of the team?

## Referral request (to someone the user knows at the company)

Structure:
1. One line reconnecting (how you know them — the user supplies this).
2. One line on the specific role and why it's a fit (real, from profile).
3. A low-friction ask: "Would you be comfortable referring me, or
   pointing me to whoever owns this req?"

## Cover note (when a posting explicitly asks for one)

Structure (3 short paragraphs, no letterhead boilerplate):
1. The role, and the one or two things about it that specifically match
   the profile — not a restatement of the resume.
2. One concrete story (a bullet from `profile.yaml`, expanded slightly
   with real context) that demonstrates the match.
3. A direct, low-key close — availability, and that the resume rides
   along with it.

## Follow-up after no response

- Wait at least 5-7 business days from the original message before
  drafting a follow-up (check `pipeline.md` for the date).
- Keep it under 50 words. Restate the ask, don't re-pitch the whole case.
- After two follow-ups with no response, suggest marking the lead cold in
  `pipeline.md` rather than drafting a third message. Some doors don't
  open twice, let alone three times.
